Abstract

We address the challenge of decoupling actions and actors to customize the text-guided diffusion model (TGDM) for few-shot action image generation. Existing methods tend to mix action-irrelevant features into the customized action images, as the few user-provided sample images lack an effective inductive bias to decouple the actions. In this work, we propose a new method, TwinAct, which disentangles actions from their associated actors in a common action space. Our core idea is to construct a common action space that is only related to actions, rather than learning customized action token embedding in an open textual embedding space, thus preventing the token embedding from being contaminated by action-irrelevant details. To be specific, we first analyze and build an action base from the embedding space of the pre-trained large text encoder. Then we generate the customized action embedding by optimizing the weight of these action bases. Additionally, we employ an action similarity loss that improves the accuracy and semantic coherence of the generated images. To comprehensively evaluate TwinAct, we collect a novel dataset, which provides various exemplar images with diverse actions. We compare TwinAct to existing customized TGDMs, demonstrating the effectiveness of our method. We can synthesize novel renditions of the user-specific action in different contexts including animals and customized actors.

Compare TwinAct with other methods. TwinAct preserves the identity consistency of actors while allowing customized actions to be accurately generalized across different actors by effectively decoupling actions and actors.
The building process of the Common Action Space. First, we collect about 832 action phrases as the initial collection, visualized on the left. Method

The overview of the proposed TwinAct. We optimize the coefficients of the action bases to avoid encoding the action-irrelevant features. After training, we combine the learned coefficients and shared action base to generate images with the customized action.
